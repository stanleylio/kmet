Where do the real-time plots get the data?

The plots are taking data directly from the logger by listening in on data broadcast (via the ZeroMQ-WebSocket relay).

Note that since the plots are not retrieving data from log files or database, they do not reflect the actual content of the database/log (i.e. the database logging process might have gone down and the plots would still update themselves). One exception, however, is when a plot is first loaded, some recent data are pulled from the database to initialize the plot.

(If more computing resource is available, I would consider generating static plots with larger time span (perhaps plot data in the past 30 days) that actually pull data from the database.)

Why does the real-time plot turns grayscale?

This signals that the plot is stale. This happens if the logger has stopped working, or if connection to the ZeroMQ-WebSocket relay has gone down.

The page turns grayscale even though new data are coming in.

The clocks on the logger and your device might be out of sync. If you sure that the clock on your device is accurate down to the seconds, notify the admin of the problem (the logger's clock might be off).
